DAA NKYEA

DAILY GREETINGS

Anͻpa (morning) yɛkyea sɛ: Mema wo akye / Maakye Awia (afternoon) yɛkyea sɛ: Mema wo aha / Maaha Anwummerɛ (evening) yɛkyea sɛ: Mema wo adwo / Maadwo NNYESOƆ - RESPONSES Wo nua / w’adamfo (age group) - yaa nua Wo maame (your mother’s age group) - yaa ɛna Wo papa (your father’s age group) - yaa agya Wo ba anaa wo nana (your child or grandchild) – yaa ͻba. DEԐ ԐSԐ SԐ YԐYԐ BERԐ A YԐREKYEA (MPANIMFOƆ) WHAT WE ARE SUPPOSE TO DO WHEN GREETING (ELDERS) 1. Tu wo kyɛ ansa na woakyea. Remove your cap before you greet 2. Yi wo nan mu mpaboa ansa na woakyea ͻhene. Remove your sandals before you greet a Chief or a Queen. 3. Kyea firi nifa so kͻ benkum. Greet from the right to the left. 4. Sɛ worekͻ tiafi a nkyea. Don’t greet when you are going to the natures call. 5. Sɛ obi redware a yɛnkyea no. We don’t greet the one who is bathing. DEԐ ENTI A YԐKYEA WHY WE GREET 1. Nkyea de ayͻnkofa ba. It brings friendship 2. Ԑkyerɛ sɛ wobu adeɛ. It shows that you respect. 3. Ԑkyerɛ sɛ wowͻ nteteɛ. It shows that you have been trained. 4. Ԑda yɛn amammerɛ adi. It shows our culture. 5. Ԑboa ma yɛnya mmoa. We get assistance from others when we greet. NNA DIN DAYS OF THE WEEK Yɛwͻ nna nson na ɛwͻ nnawͻtwe mu. There are seven days in one week. They are: NNA DIN DAYS OF THE WEEK Ԑdwoada Monday Ԑbenada Tuesday Wukuada Wednesday Yawoada Thursday Efiada Friday Memeneda Saturday Kwasiada Sunday 1. Nna num (5days) na yɛde kͻ sukuu. Yɛkͻ sukuu: Ԑdwoada, Ԑbenada, Wukuada, Yawoada ne Efiada. 2. Kristofoͻ kͻ asͻre Kwasiada ne Memeneda. Christians worship on Sundays and Saturdays. 3. Nkramofoͻ kͻ asͻre Efiada. Muslims worship on Fridays. 4. Yɛnkͻ Ԑpo Ԑbenada. We don’t go to sea on Tuesdays.
